## 1. BACKGROUND & RATIONALE

### 1.1 Clinical Context
Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a neurodevelopmental condition affecting approximately 1 in 36 children globally. Beyond core social communication and behavioral symptoms, 50-70% of individuals with ASD experience gastrointestinal (GI) symptoms including constipation, diarrhea, and abdominal pain. Emerging evidence suggests that gut microbiota dysbiosis plays a role in ASD pathogenesis through the microbiota-gut-brain axis, involving immune, neural, and metabolic pathways.

### 1.2 Scientific Rationale
Recent studies have shown that:
- Individuals with ASD exhibit distinct gut microbiota compositions compared to neurotypical controls
- Microbial dysbiosis correlates with both GI and behavioral symptom severity
- Microbiota-targeted interventions (probiotics, prebiotics, synbiotics, fecal microbiota transplantation) have demonstrated potential to ameliorate both GI dysfunction and autism-related behavioral symptoms

However, the evidence base remains fragmented across heterogeneous study designs with inconsistent outcome reporting. A systematic review is needed to synthesize the current evidence and clarify the efficacy of probiotic interventions in ASD.

### 1.3 Rationale for Systematic Review
Existing individual reviews are limited in scope or focus only on probiotics (excluding other microbiota-targeted interventions). No comprehensive PRISMA-compliant systematic review currently integrates evidence from observational studies, randomized controlled trials, and meta-analyses with standardized outcome measures and risk-of-bias assessment.

## 2. OBJECTIVES & RESEARCH QUESTION

### 2.1 Primary Objective
To systematically review and meta-analyze the efficacy of microbiota-targeted interventions (probiotics, prebiotics, synbiotics, fecal microbiota transplantation) in improving behavioral and gastrointestinal symptoms in individuals with autism spectrum disorder.

### 2.2 Research Question (PICO Format)

| Element | Definition |
|---------|-----------|
| **Population (P)** | Children and adults (age <18 and ≥18 years) with diagnosed autism spectrum disorder (per DSM-5, ICD-10, or ADOS criteria). No restrictions on ASD severity level. |
| **Intervention (I)** | Any microbiota-targeted therapeutic intervention: probiotics (single-strain or multi-strain), prebiotics, synbiotics, or fecal microbiota transplantation (FMT)/microbiota transplant therapy (MTT). Any dose, duration, strain, or formulation. |
| **Comparison (C)** | Placebo, no treatment, waitlist control, or standard care. For observational studies without explicit control: comparison to baseline measurements. |
| **Outcome (O)** | PRIMARY: Behavioral/developmental outcomes measured by validated scales (CARS, ADOS, ABC, SRS, ATEC, or similar). SECONDARY: GI symptoms (measured by GSRS, 6-GSI, or stool consistency); safety/adverse events. |
| **Study Design (S)** | Randomized controlled trials (RCTs), non-randomized controlled trials (non-RCTs), prospective cohort studies, case-control studies, open-label trials with pre-post measurement. |

### 2.3 Secondary Objectives
1. Compare efficacy across intervention types (probiotics vs. prebiotics vs. synbiotics vs. FMT)
2. Identify variables that predict intervention success (age, baseline symptom severity, intervention duration, microbiota diversity)
3. Assess quality and heterogeneity of evidence
4. Evaluate publication bias and potential reporting bias
5. Examine safety and adverse event profiles

## 3. INCLUSION CRITERIA

### 3.1 Population Inclusion
✓ **Include:**
- Individuals of any age diagnosed with autism spectrum disorder (ASD)
- Diagnosis confirmed by DSM-5, ICD-10, ADOS, or clinical diagnostic assessment by qualified professional
- Studies may include participants with comorbid conditions (ADHD, anxiety, intellectual disability, GI disorders)

✗ **Exclude:**
- Studies focusing only on relatives or family members without ASD diagnosis
- Studies including <50% participants with confirmed ASD diagnosis (unless disaggregated data available)
- Asymptomatic carriers or individuals with suspected but unconfirmed ASD

### 3.2 Intervention Inclusion
✓ **Include:**
- Probiotics: any live microorganisms (bacterial or fungal) administered as supplement
  - Single-strain formulations (e.g., *Lactobacillus plantarum* PS128)
  - Multi-strain formulations (≥2 species/strains)
  - Any dose range (0.5 billion to 900 billion CFU/day or equivalent)
  - Any administration route (oral capsule, powder, liquid)
  - Any duration of treatment (≥3 weeks)
  
- Prebiotics: non-digestible food components that promote growth of beneficial microorganisms
  - Examples: inulin, fructo-oligosaccharides (FOS), galacto-oligosaccharides (GOS), partially hydrolyzed guar gum
  - Any dose
  - Any duration (≥3 weeks)
  
- Synbiotics: combination of probiotics and prebiotics
  - Any formulation, dose, duration
  
- Fecal microbiota transplantation (FMT) / Microbiota transplant therapy (MTT)
  - Any donor type, recipient preparation, delivery method, frequency
  - Including washed microbiota transplantation (WMT) and lyophilized formulations

✗ **Exclude:**
- Pharmaceutical antibiotics or antifungals (non-prebiotic interventions)
- Dietary modifications without specific probiotic/prebiotic supplementation (e.g., "gluten-free diet alone")
- Non-microbiota targeted interventions (vitamins, behavioral therapy, standard medications)
- Studies where intervention content/dose cannot be clearly identified

### 3.3 Comparison Inclusion
✓ **Include:**
- Placebo (matched to intervention in appearance/taste when possible)
- No treatment/standard care
- Waitlist control
- Active comparators (e.g., probiotics vs. prebiotics vs. FMT)
- Baseline measurements (for pre-post observational studies without control group)

✗ **Exclude:**
- Studies with no comparison data and only post-intervention reporting

### 3.4 Outcome Inclusion
✓ **Include PRIMARY OUTCOMES:**
- Behavioral/developmental improvements measured by validated tools:
  - CARS (Childhood Autism Rating Scale) or CARS-2
  - ADOS (Autism Diagnostic Observation Schedule) or ADOS-CSS
  - ABC (Aberrant Behavior Checklist)
  - SRS (Social Responsiveness Scale) or SRS-2
  - ATEC (Autism Treatment Evaluation Checklist)
  - CGI (Clinical Global Impression Scale)
  - Other validated autism-specific behavior rating scales
  
- Measurement: change from baseline to post-intervention, between-group differences, or effect sizes

✓ **Include SECONDARY OUTCOMES:**
- GI symptom improvement measured by:
  - GSRS (Gastrointestinal Symptom Rating Scale)
  - 6-GSI or GSI (Gastrointestinal Severity Index)
  - Bristol Stool Form Scale (BSFS)
  - Other validated GI symptom scales
  
- Safety and adverse events:
  - Type, frequency, severity of adverse events
  - Discontinuation due to adverse events
  
- Microbiota composition changes:
  - Alpha diversity (Shannon, Simpson, Chao1)
  - Beta diversity metrics
  - Relative abundance of specific taxa

✗ **Exclude:**
- Studies measuring only microbiota changes without behavioral/GI outcomes
- Biomarker-only studies (e.g., inflammatory markers without symptom measurement)
- Studies with non-validated outcome measures or qualitative descriptions without quantification

### 3.5 Study Design Inclusion
✓ **Include:**
- Randomized controlled trials (RCTs) - parallel group or crossover design
- Non-randomized controlled trials (quasi-experimental designs)
- Prospective cohort studies with comparison group
- Case-control studies
- Open-label trials with pre-post measurement and ≥20 participants
- Blinded or unblinded designs

**Minimum participant requirement:** ≥15 participants in primary analysis (to minimize selection bias in small studies)

✗ **Exclude:**
- Case reports (n<5)
- Case series (<15 participants)
- Uncontrolled retrospective studies
- Studies with unclear sample size or data
- Conference abstracts without subsequent full publication (unless unpublished data requested from authors)

### 3.6 Language & Publication Inclusion
✓ **Include:**
- English-language publications
- Peer-reviewed journal articles
- Doctoral dissertations and theses (indexed in academic databases)
- Clinical trial reports (registered and completed)

✗ **Exclude:**
- Non-English language publications (resource constraint; English fluency only)
- Opinion pieces, editorials, narrative reviews
- Unpublished manuscripts or preprints (unless registered clinical trial with available results)
- Books and book chapters

### 3.7 Temporal & Geographic Inclusion
✓ **Include:**
- Studies published between January 1, 2015 and December 31, 2025
- Rationale: 2015 marks the shift toward microbiota-gut-brain axis research in ASD
- Any geographic location/country

✗ **Exclude:**
- Studies published before 2015
- Studies with baseline data collected before 2010 (to exclude older cohorts with different diagnostic criteria)

## 4. EXCLUSION CRITERIA (Summary)

### Specific Exclusion Categories:
1. **Population:** <50% confirmed ASD diagnosis (unless data disaggregated)
2. **Intervention:** Non-microbiota interventions; unspecified formulations
3. **Outcome:** No behavioral or GI symptom measurement
4. **Study design:** Case reports/series (<15 participants); uncontrolled retrospective studies
5. **Sample size:** <15 participants in primary analysis
6. **Language:** Non-English publications
7. **Publication type:** Opinion, editorial, narrative review without original data
8. **Date:** Published before 2015 or baseline data pre-2010
9. **Quality:** Insufficient outcome data to calculate effect size or compare groups
10. **Duplicates:** Same dataset published multiple times (retain most recent/complete)

## 5. STUDY DESIGN FILTER & HIERARCHY

### 5.1 Study Design Hierarchy (by strength of evidence)
1. **Tier 1 (Highest):** Randomized controlled trials with:
   - Proper randomization method reported
   - Allocation concealment
   - Blinded participants/assessors
   - Intention-to-treat analysis
   
2. **Tier 2 (High):** Randomized controlled trials with:
   - Some bias concerns (e.g., no blinding) but otherwise rigorous
   
3. **Tier 3 (Moderate):** Non-randomized controlled trials with:
   - Clearly defined comparison groups
   - Pre-post measurement
   
4. **Tier 4 (Lower):** Prospective cohort or open-label trials with:
   - Pre-post measurement
   - ≥15 participants
   - Baseline characteristics reported
   
5. **Tier 5 (Lowest):** Retrospective case-control studies
   - Only included if no prospective data available

### 5.2 Analysis Strategy by Study Design
- **Primary analysis:** Pool Tier 1-2 RCT data via meta-analysis
- **Secondary analysis:** Include Tier 3-4 designs separately (sensitivity analysis)
- **Tertiary analysis:** Subgroup analyses restricted to Tier 1-2 data only

## 6. OUTCOME MEASUREMENT & DEFINITION

### 6.1 Primary Outcome: Behavioral Improvement in ASD Symptoms
**Measurement Tools (any of the following):**
- CARS/CARS-2: 15-item scale, scored 15-60 (lower = less severe autism)
- ADOS/ADOS-CSS: Module-dependent; CSS scored 1-10 (lower = less severe)
- ABC: 58-item scale, scored 0-174 (lower = fewer aberrant behaviors)
- SRS/SRS-2: 65-item scale, T-scores standardized (lower = fewer social impairments)
- ATEC: 77-item scale, scored 0-179 (lower = fewer autism-related problems)
- CGI-I: 7-point scale, scored 1-7 (lower = greater improvement)

**Reporting:**
- Change from baseline to post-intervention
- Between-group differences (intervention vs. control)
- Effect sizes (Cohen's d, standardized mean difference)

### 6.2 Secondary Outcome: GI Symptom Improvement
**Measurement Tools (any of the following):**
- GSRS: 15-item scale, scored 0-45 (lower = fewer GI symptoms)
- 6-GSI: Composite score (lower = fewer GI symptoms)
- BSFS: 7-point stool form scale (Target = Type 3-4 = normal consistency)
- QPGS-RIII: Rome III questionnaire
- Symptom-specific: Frequency of constipation, diarrhea, abdominal pain

**Reporting:**
- Change in GI symptom severity
- Change in stool consistency
- Resolution of specific GI complaints

### 6.3 Tertiary Outcome: Safety & Adverse Events
**Definition:** Any unintended physical or psychological effect attributed to intervention

**Categorization:**
- Severity: Mild (transient, doesn't affect function), Moderate (interferes with function), Severe (hospitalization required)
- Causality: Definitely related, Probably related, Possibly related, Unlikely related
- Type: GI-related (diarrhea, constipation, bloating), Behavioral (hyperactivity, aggression), Systemic (fever, rash), Other

**Reporting:**
- Number and percentage of participants experiencing AE
- Type and severity distribution
- Discontinuation due to AE

## 8. QUALITY ASSESSMENT

### 8.1 Risk of Bias Assessment Tools
**For RCTs:** Cochrane Risk of Bias Tool 2 (RoB 2)
- Domains: Randomization process, Deviation from intended intervention, Missing outcome data, Measurement of outcome, Selection of reported result
- Rating: Low risk, Some concerns, High risk

**For Non-RCTs:** ROBINS-I (Risk of Bias in Non-randomized Studies)
- Domains: Confounding, Selection bias, Intervention classification, Deviations, Missing data, Outcome measurement, Selection of reported results
- Rating: Low risk, Moderate risk, Serious risk, Critical risk

**For Observational Studies:** Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS)
- Domains: Selection (4 points), Comparability (2 points), Outcome/Exposure (3 points)
- Threshold: ≥6/9 points considered good quality

### 8.2 Quality Assessment Procedure
- Independent assessment by 2 reviewers
- Disagreements resolved by consensus or third reviewer
- Graphical presentation of risk of bias across studies
- Sensitivity analysis excluding high-risk studies

## 9. STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN (Summary)

### 9.1 Primary Meta-Analysis
**Effect size metric:** Standardized mean difference (Cohen's d)
- Rationale: Allows pooling across different outcome scales (CARS, ABC, SRS, etc.)
- Interpretation: 0.2=small, 0.5=medium, 0.8=large

**Model:** Random-effects (DerSimonian-Laird) to account for expected heterogeneity

**Heterogeneity assessment:** 
- I² statistic (0%=no heterogeneity, 100%=complete heterogeneity)
- τ² (tau-squared, between-study variance)
- Interpretation: I²<25% low, 25-50% moderate, 50-75% high, >75% very high

**Subgroup analyses:**
- By intervention type (probiotics vs. prebiotics vs. synbiotics vs. FMT)
- By population age (children <18 vs. adults ≥18)
- By study design (RCTs only vs. including non-RCTs)
- By outcome measurement tool (CARS vs. ABC vs. SRS vs. ATEC)

### 9.2 Publication Bias Assessment
- Funnel plot inspection (asymmetry suggests bias)
- Egger's regression test (p<0.05 suggests bias)
- Interpretation: Acknowledge if detected; discuss implications

### 9.3 Sensitivity Analyses
- Excluding studies with high risk of bias
- Excluding outlier studies
- Leave-one-out analysis
- Fixed vs. random effects comparison
